Before You Arrive

Know the Lore

No spoilers. Just enough to make Middle-earth feel like home before you step into it.

The World

Middle-earth is an ancient world created by a god-like being called Ilúvatar — shaped through music and will before the first sunrise. It is a world in its Third Age: old, scarred by great wars, and full of peoples who carry long memories. The films take place at the end of this era, when something must be decided once and for all.

The Shire

A small, peaceful corner of the northwest — rolling green hills, round doors, garden plots, and absolutely no interest in adventure. Home of the Hobbits. The films begin here, and the contrast between this gentle world and everything beyond its borders is very much the point.

Rivendell

An Elven refuge hidden in a river valley — ancient, beautiful, and untouched by the noise of the world outside. The Elves who live there are among the oldest living beings in Middle-earth. Rivendell is a place of counsel, memory, and a certain aching loveliness.

Mordor

The land of shadow in the east — volcanic, barren, and ruled by a darkness that has been building for thousands of years. You will not want to go there. The characters do not want to go there either.

The One Ring

Forged in secret by a Dark Lord to control all other rings of power — and through them, the peoples of Middle-earth. It was lost, found again, and now sits in the pocket of a hobbit who doesn't entirely know what he has. The films' prologue tells you everything you need to know about its history.

The Languages

Tolkien was a linguist who built Middle-earth around its languages. Elvish exists in two forms: Quenya (high and ceremonial) and Sindarin (everyday). Dwarves speak Khuzdul, which they guard jealously. The Black Speech of Mordor is inscribed on the Ring itself. They are real, fully constructed languages with grammar and vocabulary.

Quick Glossary
The FellowshipA company of nine — drawn from the free peoples of Middle-earth — formed to share the burden of the quest.
IstariThe wizards. There are five, though you'll meet two. They are not human — they are ancient beings sent to guide, not to rule.
NazgûlThe Ringwraiths — nine kings of Men who accepted rings of power and were consumed by them. They serve the Dark Lord now and pursue the One Ring relentlessly.
MithrilA rare, incredibly strong silver metal found in the Dwarven mines. Worth more than all the Shire.
LembasElvish waybread — light, nourishing, and one piece is enough to fill the stomach of a grown man. The Hobbits eat several.
EntsThe shepherds of the forest — ancient tree-beings, slow to speak and very slow to anger. When they are finally angry, it is worth the wait.
Second BreakfastA Hobbit meal between breakfast and elevenses. Non-negotiable. Yes, this becomes a plot point.
Come as You Are

Costume Guide

Dress as your people — whoever the quiz says you are, or whoever you feel in your bones. From zero spend to a little effort, here's how.

Hobbit
Free / Already Own
  • Go barefoot — this is non-negotiable
  • White or cream loose shirt
  • Suspenders over a vest if you have one
  • Rolled-up, slightly too-short trousers
  • Rosy cheeks via blush
  • Curly hair encouraged — let it be free
Mid-Tier ($15–40)
  • Hobbit foot covers (Amazon, ~$15–25)
  • Full waistcoat from a thrift store
  • Pipe prop (no tobacco required)
Elf
Free / Already Own
  • Long flowy dress, tunic, or draped fabric in cream, silver, or forest green
  • Silver or leaf-shaped jewelry you already own
  • Braided crown hairstyle
  • Drawn ear point with makeup at the tip
  • Regal posture — this is the whole costume
Mid-Tier ($10–35)
  • Silicone elf ear tips (~$8–12)
  • Elvish leaf brooch or circlet (Etsy or Amazon)
  • Flowy kimono repurposed as Elvish robes
Dwarf
Free / Already Own
  • Heaviest, most layered outfit you own
  • Belt with a large buckle
  • Boots — the chunkier the better
  • Braid your beard or your hair, or draw one on
  • Attitude of absolute refusal to be impressed
Mid-Tier ($15–40)
  • Fake beard and braid kit (~$12–20)
  • Fur-trimmed vest from thrift
  • Foam axe prop (~$10–15)
Wizard
Free / Already Own
  • Any long robe, cloak, or oversized cardigan — grey or white preferred
  • A walking stick or broom handle as a staff
  • Wide-brim hat if you have one
  • Drawn-on bushy brows and a weathered expression
  • Speak in slow, weighted sentences all evening
Mid-Tier ($20–45)
  • Grey or white wizard robe (Amazon)
  • Gnarled wooden staff prop
  • White wig and beard if going full Gandalf
Ranger / Man
Free / Already Own
  • Dark, layered clothing — worn and weathered looking
  • Any hooded cloak or dark blanket over the shoulders
  • Boots
  • Look like you've traveled far and slept little
  • Brooding expression optional but encouraged
Mid-Tier ($20–50)
  • Ranger cloak or dark wool cape from thrift
  • Leather bracers (~$15–25)
  • Prop sword or scabbard
Ent
Free / Already Own
  • Earthy brown and green layers
  • Tuck leaves, twigs, and small branches into your clothing and hair
  • Move very, very slowly all evening
  • Respond to all questions after a long pause
Mid-Tier ($15–30)
  • Faux vine and leaf garland woven through outfit (craft store)
  • Face paint in bark-brown and green tones
  • Branch walking stick, heavily decorated
Orc
Free / Already Own
  • Dark, tattered clothing — the more destroyed the better
  • Face paint in gray, green, or dark tones
  • Scowl that doesn't quit
  • Eat aggressively. Question nothing.
Mid-Tier ($15–35)
  • Orc prosthetic nose or brow piece (~$15–25)
  • Armor-look accessories from costume shop or thrift
  • Stage makeup kit for full skin coverage
